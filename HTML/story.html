大正十一年六月 梅雨の神保町

会津から上京してきた正太朗は、帝大合格を目指す浪人生.
しかし底知れない空想癖とだらしない性格が災いし、たった二年で下宿先に見限られてしまう。
とある縁で古書店・梅鉢堂に住み込みで働くことになった正太朗は、
同郷の親友らに甘えながらも浪人生という猶予期間を謳歌していた。

そんな中、頼りにしていた親友の相次ぐ自殺と怪死。
雨の降る三日間をなぜか繰り返していることに気づいた正太朗は、
彼らを掬うため神保町を奔走する。

何が現実で、
何が幻覚なのか。

友人の死の謎を追う、ポップオカルトな大正ミステリー

In June 1918
Jimbocho in the rainy season

Shotaro came to Tokyo with his sights set on getting accepted
to the Imperial University.
However, he doesn't pass the entrance exam, and is so undisciplined and wrapped up in endless fantasies that he's kicked out of
his boarding house after only two years.

Through a stroke of luck,
Shotaro begins to live and work at Umebachidou, a used book store.
There, he enjoys whiling away his time as a
"student preparing for entrance exams"
and taking advantage of friends who are also from his hometown.

But one right after the other,
mysterious deaths befall those friends
and, realizing he's been repeating the same rainy three days
over and over again,
Shotaro finds himself racing throughout Jinbochou to save them.

What is real,
what is a hallucination?

In this pop-occult mystery that surrounds the deaths of his friends
